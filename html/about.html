Swimming In Data

About Me


Hi, I'm Kathy and this website is about how we as a society are collecting and processing increasingly greater amounts of data--or information--about every aspect of our lives.


"Perfect information" (a state of the world in which people can easily and accurately obtain information) is essential for a well functioning market economy.

In some ways, the development of the web has given us an unprecedented ability to access information. The proliferation of websites dedicated to online learning for youth, such as Khan Academy, and the multitude of sites where you are able to learn from world-class professors--moocs such as Edx and Coursera, free online courses provided by University of Michigan's Dr. Chuck, and youtube channels, such as John Hopkin's Roger Peng teaching R, NYU's Daniel Siffman teaching Processing at his channel The Coding Train, as well as Chris Ching teaching Swift at CodeWithChris --are just a few examples that I have personally used.

Search engines such as Google allow us to find and compare products and services easily. I am no longer surprised when I imagine a new product idea, and can invariably I can find the product already for sale on the web.

All of this amazing technology comes with a cost. The price is the marketing data that is being collected about us. I am going to explore how this information is currently being collected and used--for good or ill--and how you can make informed decisions based on the available data.
